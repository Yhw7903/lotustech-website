Notice — ATCC Taxonomic Name Changes (USP–NF, 18 Dec 2025): The American Type Culture Collection (ATCC) has implemented taxonomic updates for certain reference strains cited in the USP–NF, reflecting advances in phylogenetic and genomic research. Bacillus subtilis (ATCC 6633) is now designated Bacillus spizizenii (formerly Bacillus subtilis); Pseudomonas aeruginosa (ATCC 9027) is now designated Pseudomonas paraeruginosa (formerly Pseudomonas aeruginosa). NCTC = National Collection of Type Cultures  |  NCPF = National Collection of Pathogenic Fungi  |  ATCC = American Type Culture Collection  |  WDCM = World Data Centre for Microorganisms.
NCTC and NCPF are trade marks of UKHSA. Both collections are held by the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA, Porton Down).
ATCC® is a registered trademark of the American Type Culture Collection. ATCC strain designations are cited solely to indicate equivalence and are not intended to imply any affiliation with or endorsement by ATCC.
VerisQ products are licensed for non-therapeutic pharmaceutical quality control testing only, as defined under the UKHSA Culture Collections licence agreement.

Pharmacopoeial basis for equivalence: USP <1117>, EP 5.1.4 / 5.1.5 / 5.1.6, and JP General Information <G4> permit use of strains from alternative culture collections that can be demonstrated to be equivalent ("or equivalent strain" / "or strains shown to be equivalent"). NCTC and NCPF strains share the same original isolation source, phenotypic and genotypic characteristics, and WDCM registration number as their ATCC counterparts, and are internationally recognised as interchangeable reference strains.
