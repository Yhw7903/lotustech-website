Pharmacopoeial Equivalence with ATCC
NCTC and NCPF strains are accepted as equivalent to ATCC under USP, EP and JP.

The United States Pharmacopeia (USP), European Pharmacopoeia (EP) and Japanese Pharmacopoeia (JP) routinely cite reference cultures by ATCC catalogue number. Each VerisQ™ strain shares an identical taxonomic pedigree, phenotypic and genotypic profile, and a common World Data Centre for Microorganisms (WDCM) identifier with the corresponding ATCC strain. UKHSA Culture Collections formally recognises this equivalence, and pharmacopoeia users may rely on these strains under the "or strains shown to be equivalent" allowance in USP <60>, <61>, <62>, <71>, EP 5.1.3 and the corresponding JP general chapters.

A signed Strain Equivalence Statement (.docx) is provided for every licensed strain — use the Statement column below to download the document for your QA file. Each statement records the NCTC/NCPF, ATCC and WDCM identifiers, the pharmacopoeial citations, source traceability to BE25 009, and limits of use, and is issued under the LotusTech corporate stamp.

Note: Salmonella enterica serovar Abony NCTC 6017 has no direct ATCC homologue and is cited verbatim under its NCTC number in USP <62> and EP 2.6.13. Burkholderia multivorans NCTC 13007 is the species type strain (= ATCC BAA-247 = LMG 13010 = DSM 13243).
